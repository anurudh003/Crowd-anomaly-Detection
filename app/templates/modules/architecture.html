Spatio-Temporal Anomaly Detection

Module 1

A CNN-LSTM Autoencoder trained on the UCSD Pedestrian Ped2 dataset learns the normal distribution of crowd motion. At inference time, the reconstruction error of an 8-frame sliding window (stride=4) serves as the raw anomaly score. CLAHE pre-processing and Gaussian blur reduce sensor noise before the MobileNetV2 backbone encodes spatial features. A 2-layer LSTM (hidden=256) captures temporal dependencies across the sequence.

Optical-Flow Density Heatmap

Module 2

Frame-differencing accumulates a motion energy map over a 30-frame history window. Morphological operations (kernel=5) clean noise. Gaussian blur (kernel=15) produces smooth density gradients. The JET colormap overlay (blue → red) visualises crowd pressure zones in real-time. The density score is the normalized pixel-sum of the heatmap, thresholded at 0.75 for overcrowding alerts.

Abandoned Object Detection

Module 3

YOLOv5n runs every 10 processed frames on a 320×240 downscaled frame for speed. A centroid-based tracker classifies objects as stationary if pixel displacement ≤ 20px. Items stationary for ≥ 30 seconds trigger an ABANDONED_OBJECT alert. Vehicles (car, bus, motorcycle, etc.) also directly boost the CNN-LSTM blended anomaly score by +1.0.
